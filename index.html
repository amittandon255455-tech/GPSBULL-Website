FAQ

Questions fleets ask us

AIS-140 is the Government of India standard for vehicle location tracking devices with emergency buttons. It is mandatory for public service vehicles and national-permit commercial vehicles in most states. Our device carries a 4G VLTD Type Approval Certificate and is registered with state backends.

Devices use GPS, GLONASS and IRNSS constellations together, giving typical accuracy of 2.5 metres in open sky. Update intervals are configurable from 10 seconds for live tracking down to 1 second for special applications.

Yes. Devices buffer up to thousands of position records in internal memory when the network drops and replay them automatically when connectivity returns, so trip history is never lost. 4G devices fall back to 2G where available.

Yes. The platform exposes REST APIs and webhooks for positions, alerts and reports. We also support direct device-data forwarding to your servers for OEM and white-label arrangements.

A wired GPS tracker installs in 30–45 minutes per vehicle; OBD devices plug in within minutes. For large fleets we run scheduled installation camps at your depots so vehicles never miss a duty cycle.

Data travels encrypted from device to platform, is stored in Indian data centres, and access is role-based with full audit logs. We are ISO 9001:2015 certified and follow CMMI Level 3 software practices.

Yes. Devices ship with embedded multi-operator eSIMs (mandatory for AIS-140) or standard M2M SIMs, and we manage activation, data plans and renewals so you have a single vendor for the whole stack.

Standard warranty is one year with extended options. Support runs 24×7 over phone and email, with advance-replacement SLAs for enterprise fleets so a failed unit never grounds a vehicle.
